Experience

At Vanderbilt, I actively participated in academic research and explored machine learning's application in Emergency Medical Services, Self-Driving Cars, and VR Maze Analysis. I was selected for Honorable Mention for CRA 2021 Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Award.

Medical Procedures
In Summer 2019, I joined Prof. Bodenheimer's lab and coded in Python to develop a video pipeline that automatically recognizes clinical procedures and sends real-time information to hospitals during emergencies. The results were summerized in the paper "Emergency Clinical Procedure Detection with Deep Learning."
Self-Driving Cars
In Summer 2020, I interned at the Institute for Software Integrated Systems and coded in python to build the ALC Toolchain. Now, I am working with Prof. Dubey and a Ph.D. student to build a system to defend against adversarial attacks on autonomous driving environments.
VR Maze
In Spring 2020, I did independent research with Prof. Bodenheimer. I applied 3 dimensionality reduction methods (UMAP, t-SNE, and PCA) with k-means and discovered 2 clusters with a significant difference in MRT score, which indicates a connection between mental rotation ability and how people learned spatial layouts.
